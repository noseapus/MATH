**Definition:** Let $X$ be a [random variable](/D/rvar). Then, $X$ is said to be standard normally distributed, if $X$ follows a [normal distribution](/D/norm) with mean $\mu = 0$ and variance $\sigma^2 = 1$:

$$ \label{eq:snorm}
X \sim \mathcal{N}(0, 1) \; .
$$
